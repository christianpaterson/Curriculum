Atoms and Electrons ⚛️

Yeah so we’ve got atoms, which are what they sound like as long as you’ve made it past 7th grade. The organization of a typical atom has a nucleus of protons and neutrons, and electrons orbiting outside, probabilistically located somewhere within different energy levels, or orbitals. Electrons are far less massive (1/1836) than protons. They have higher energy (and thus greater velocity) the farther they are from the nucleus. Electrons have electric potential energy due to their charge, lower when they are closer to the nucleus, and more when they are farther away. Charged particles create an electric field around themselves. This field exerts forces on other charged particles within its influence. The electric potential energy of a charged particle (electron or proton) is determined by its position within this electric field. The energy decreases as the particle moves farther from similarly charged particles and closer to oppositely charged particles. This energy depends on the charge of the particle, the distance between charges, and the strength of the electric field. Electrons exist in discrete energy levels around the nucleus of an atom, which are defined by quantum mechanics. They are most likely found in regions called orbitals, and their precise positions cannot be determined exactly. The electrons moving throughout whatever conducting material are the valence electrons (the same ones which form covalent bonds in other contexts). If current is flowing, some electrons in the outer shells of atoms are moving through the material. Metals overall will still maintain a neutral charge though (almost always, and if not, then it is usually only brief moments that they as a whole are charged). So there are NOT usually more electrons within the metal than could theoretically be captured within all the atoms of the metal. But there very well may be more free electrons than electrons associating with one particular atom. Put another way, while there are more free electrons than protons in a localized region within a metal when an electric field is applied, this does not imply that the entire metal has a net negative charge (static electricity). Electrons continuously move and redistribute themselves as the current flows, but the overall balance of charge within the metal remains neutral (charge conservation).
